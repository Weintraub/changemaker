Solution Landscape

Although there are a variety of solutions that tackle many of the small portions of this large and complicated issue, three of the largest categories of solutions focus on reversing and preventing habitat destruction, reducing water pollution, and combating unsustainable fishing.

Efforts

The Kwikwetlem First Nations have contributed a great deal to this issue through the Wilson Farm Project, a restoration project to reverse the effects of water pollution and prevent future damage to these rivers and streams. Their work involves monitoring progress through methods such as fish and egg presence and water quality.

Currently, the Department of Fisheries Ontario is partnering with communities through recreational fisheries conservation partnership programs in order to monitor what is caught and enforce catch regulations. By placing limits on the number of fish that can be caught, the population has a chance to spawn and regrow, and thus maintain a healthy size.

The Pacific Salmon Foundation (PSF) will also receive $1.2 million over three years through the CRF. With this funding, the PSF will be able to restore the aquatic ecosystems of Pacific wild salmon (and the myriad of species they support), as well as expand key salmon-bearing watersheds in British Columbia.
