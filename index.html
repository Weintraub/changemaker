Map the System

"Tackling global challenges starts with understanding a problem and its wider context, rather than jumping straight into a business plan or an idea for a quick fix."

Our goal was initially to look into the unsustainability of the salmon farming industry in British Columbia. However, through our research, in addition to speaking with the government and local tribes, we've discovered that the issue stems beyond unsustainable practice. Indigenous communities are not recieving the acknowledgement and respect that they deserve. With no simple solution in sight we began with looking at the problem and solution landscape, followed by identifying the gaps & levers of change.

History, Scope, and Size

Problem Landscape

There is a history of unsustainability and social injustice in the fishing industry, especially when aiming to maintain the status quo. With minimal awareness raised the inidgenous communities of British Columbia suffer. The scope of issue concerns not only subsistence for Indigenous people, but extends to a sense of cultural identity.

History, Efforts, and Future

Solution Landscape

There are various efforts being made towards solving and eliminating the issue of salmon populations in BC, with contributions being made from a variety of groups including from the government, climate activists, and Indigenous communities. While not every solution has succeeded, there is a positive trajectory in hopes of a better future.

Market Gaps, Opportunities, and Responsibility

Gaps & Levers of Change

While organizations and groups are trying their best to solve a problem, each individual tribe has a unique problem that is slightly different. Additionally, while efforts are often directed towards the salmon populations themselves, these solutions fail to take into account the unique Aboriginal perspective on the issue. Unfortunately, this causes gaps in the solutions landscape; however, there are many market opportunities to fill these gaps, and the future looks bright.
